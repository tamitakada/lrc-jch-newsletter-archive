by Tami Takada

Tokyo has only about one sixteenth of Osaka’s special education programs for students with developmental disabilities and other special needs. Most Tokyo schools have limited programs that operate just several hours a week for special needs students, but few places offer full-time support. Expanding special education programs could be one key to solving Japan’s overall school phobia issue.

[däipäŋ]—the act of hitting one’s table in frustration, typically from a video game or pachinko (pinball machine for gambling). Although frequent enough to become a word on its own, it is viewed as rude and can lead to property damage.
「子供のころ、しょぼんのアクションで死んでよく台パンしてたなー」
“As a kid, I used to rage smash my desk all the time from dying in Cat Mario…”
「あんま台パンしてると器物損壊罪で捕まんぞ」
“If you keep on hitting the machine you’re gonna be arrested for property damage”
