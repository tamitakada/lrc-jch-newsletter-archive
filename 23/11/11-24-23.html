by Tami Takada

Japan’s host club industry has been under fire for manipulating women into prostitution. Hosts have been known to offer reduced club entry fees to socially vulnerable women to rope them into prostitution. As a result, it has been found that over 40% of women arrested for prostitution needed the money to pay back debt owed to the hosts that had brought them into the industry.

[käwät͡ɕiː]—a modern synonym for かわいい [käwäiː], meaning “cute” or “adorable.” Although there is no definitive origin, it interestingly correlates to the popularity of the hit manga series titled ちいかわ [t͡ɕiːkäwä], an abbreviation for なんか小さくてかわいいやつ, or “something small and cute,” with 小さい [t͡ɕiːsäi] meaning “small.”
「このかわちい生きものを見てくれ！！」
“Look at this adorable creature!!”
「ダイソーのポーチ……かわちい……最高…」
“Small makeup bag from DAISO… So cute… I love it…”

Every year, booksellers around Japan vote on the top 10 books of the year that they are most excited to sell for the 本屋大賞 (Honyataisho), or Bookseller’s Award. This year’s winner was Nagira Yu’s romance novel 「汝、星の如く」(Nanji, Hoshi no Gotoku). Yu is a repeat winner from her 2020 romance「流浪の月」(Rurou no Tsuki), which was made into the movie Wandering (2022).

Highlights from past years:

  • 2018 winner「かがみの孤城」 (available in English as Lonely Castle in the Mirror) by Tsujimura Mizuki
  • 2017 winner「蜜蜂と遠雷」(Mitsubachi to Enrai) by Onda Riku
  • 2004 winner 「博士の愛した数式」(available in English as The Housekeeper and the Professor) by Ogawa Yoko
