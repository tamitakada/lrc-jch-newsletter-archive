[ɕiɾäŋke̞do̞]—A saying common in the Kansai dialect used after an utterance to express uncertainty in their prior utterance. It can be used to emphasize uncertainty, backpedal on claims, or avoid responsibility.
「お隣さん、イギリス人だって。知らんけど。」
“Our neighbor is British. Or so I’ve heard.”
「二番目でいいんじゃね？知らんけど。」
“The second option’s fine… Probably.”

This week's book recommendation is Oosaki Yoshio's Satoshi no Seishun (聖の青春). Oosaki discusses the life of shogi prodigy Murayama Satoshi (村山聖), who reached the top rank of shogi despite battling chronic illness throughout. Oosaki, who knew Murayama personally, offers a unique, personal insight into Satoshi's journey from first contracting illness to battling shogi legend Habu Yoshiharu to his tragic death at the age of 29. Satoshi no Seishun was also turned into a movie, although the movie barely holds a candle to the book (in this writer's opinion).
