Japan has the 2nd most planetariums out of all countries; this year marks the 100th anniversary of the birth of the modern planetarium. The Nagoya City Science Museum has the most visitors in all of Japan with the largest planetarium having a diameter of 35 meters, using a joint 700 panels, with a maximum capacity of 350 visitors at once. Cougar Hashikura is one of the frequent freshman participants of Japanese Conversation Hour. Here are a few questions he answered for this week’s newsletter:

Tell us about your background/experience with Japanese.
"I am fully Japanese but I was born in America, so my exposure to the language and the culture has been somewhat limited. That’s why I’m currently taking Japanese classes and engaging in Japanese media to get back in touch!"

What are you majoring in?
"I am majoring in CS, with a possible minor in psychology. I really enjoy coding projects for useful purposes, and I want to learn more about psychology to help create better mental systems for students."

What is your favorite Japanese food?
"My favorite Japanese food has to be Ikura sushi. Ikura is already one of my favorite food items in general, but it’s really something else even with something as basic as rice and seaweed!"

What kind of alcohol can annoy your enemies?

Hint: Enemy = 敵 (teki), Annoyed = イライラ (ira ira) in Japanese.
