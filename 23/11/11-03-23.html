by Tami Takada

On October 11th, shogi prodigy Souta Fujii (藤井聡太) achieved the first 8-crown grand slam of Shogi (八冠) at just 21 years old. Previous shogi players had only achieved up to 7 crowns, although this was due in part to the newest eiou (叡王) title being added relatively recently in 2015. Fujii won after discovering a game-changing move that even AI had failed to foresee.

Sara Goto is one of the Japanese Conversation Hour’s freshman facilitators. She mastered Japanese at a young age despite never having lived in Japan before. Here are a few questions she answered for this week’s newsletter:

Tell us about your background/experience with Japanese.
"I was born and raised in America, but since I never went outside my first language was Japanese. And since I still don’t go outside I still have the same proficiency in both languages.

My hobbies are all related to Japanese media in some way [shocked expression]. So, I really like talking about it and teaching people about it.

What are you majoring in?
“I’m a prospective statistical science major and linguistics minor. My high school didn’t allow me to take statistics in sophomore year, so I got really angry and I’m continuing it out of spite.”

What are your hobbies?
“Music, visual arts, and Internet.”

Do you have anything else you’d like to share?
