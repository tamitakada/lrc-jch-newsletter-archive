by Tami Takada

Rie Qudan (九段理江), winner of last month’s Akutagawa prize, used ChatGPT to come up with the idea for her winning book, Tōkyō-to Dōjōtō (東京都同情塔). Dōjōtō follows the life of an architect as she builds a tower for former criminals in Shinjuku while relying on help from generative AI like ChatGPT. Qudan revealed that she relied on ChatGPT for editing advice and feedback on the flow of her paragraphs as she was writing the novel, although she maintains that ChatGPT is unable to recreate the adaptability of human language.
