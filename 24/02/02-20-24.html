by Bora Shin

“ぞっとする” means to “shudder, shiver, or tremble due to something horrifying.” While the presence of “ない[nai]” in “ぞっとしない” indeed suggests a negation of the feeling described by “ぞっとする,” “ぞっとしない” is not an opposite term of “ぞっとする.” Rather, “ぞっとしない” has a meaning of “not very appealing(感心しない)” or "does not evoke strong emotion(強い感動を受けない)," and thus sometimes, "not so interesting(面白くない)." Therefore, you cannot use the expression “ぞっとしない” to say that you are not scared.

「宿題を忘れてぞっとした。」
“I got really scared because I forgot my homework”
「有名な映画を見たが、私はぞっとしなかった。」
“Although I watched a famous film, I was not so drawn to it.”
