by Tami Takada

The Ainu people once controlled much of Northern Japan, but by the early Edo period, they had little say against the unfair trade regulations imposed upon them by the Japanese. In 1668, discontented Ainu revolted against the Japanese. They were led by Shakushain, who instigated riots that left 273 Japanese civilians dead. The revolt was finally put down in 1671 after the Japanese representatives of Matsumae-han murdered Shakushain and fourteen other leaders during a peace talk.

Toshihiko Takano, Tenka taihei no jidai (Tokyo: Iwanami Shoten, 2015), 21-24.

by Bora Shin

‘Mirai’ and ‘syourai’ would both be translated as “future” in English; yet, their usage and nuance differs slightly. While ‘mirai’ is an objective term which corresponds to the “future” that chronologically comes at any point of time after the present, ‘syourai’ refers to an exclusively personal and far “future”. The use of ‘syourai’ and ‘mirai’ are not interchangeable, and in cases where ‘syourai’ would be appropriate, ‘mirai’ would not be.
