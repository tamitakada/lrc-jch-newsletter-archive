Yamamoto’s design philosophy centers on community-building, which includes thinking of how design can service different kinds of groups—from local fishermen to firefighters to tourists—in a community. by Bora Shin

役不足[yakubusoku] is often misunderstood as implying "someone lacking the capability for a particular task or job (自分の能力に対して役目が重い)." However, the more suitable term for this interpretation is 力不足[chikarabusoku]. Instead, 役不足 actually denotes the opposite: it signifies "the task or job not being appropriate for someone, as it is too easy for them (その人の能力・力量に対し、与えられた役目が不相応に軽いこと)." While both 役不足 and 力不足 indicate a mismatch between a role and a person, they do so in opposite ways.
「彼女はとても優秀なので、その職務では役不足だ」
“As she is very capable, this role does not fit her.” (task < capability)
「リーダーを務めるには、彼はまだ力不足だろう」
“He is probably not yet capable of being the leader.” (task > capability)
